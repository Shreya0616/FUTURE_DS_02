Customer Retention and Churn Analysis

Objective:
Analyze customer behavior to identify churn patterns, retention drivers, and customer lifetime trends for a subscription based business.

Tools Used:
Power BI
Microsoft Excel

Dataset:
500 customers
Variables include: Subscription plan, tenure, usage frequency, payment failures, revenue, churn status.

Key Metrics:
Total Customers: 500
Churn Rate: 9.6 percent
Average Tenure: 12.83 months
Customer Lifetime Value: 6.76K

Key Insights:
Churn rate is 9.6 percent overall.
Standard plan shows higher churn compared to Premium.
Low usage customers show significantly higher churn.
First 3 months show higher customer drop off.
Premium customers generate highest revenue with better retention.

Retention Drivers:
Usage frequency
Tenure duration
Subscription plan

Business Recommendations:
Improve onboarding experience in first 90 days.
Run engagement campaigns for low usage users.
Encourage upgrade to Premium plan.
Introduce early churn risk alerts for low engagement users.

Business Impact
Reducing churn by even 2 to 3 percent can significantly increase customer lifetime value and long term revenue stability.
